## Comparing the spinups

All the spinups yield reasonable error metrics => the decision of which to go with should rather be taken based on their behaviour on AQEF experiment.

I made some corner sampling => parameters with extreme values:
- 0: H1 = 50; delta = 0.2. V at tip: 52 msle. f at tip: 2K. Amundsen collapse happens too late and is synced with Ross collapse.
- 6: H1 = 50; delta = 0.8. V at tip: 56 msle. f at tip: 1.6K. Amundsen collapses in very clean way and propagates to Ross subsequently.
- 7: H1 = 100; delta = 0.2. V at tip: 53 msle. f at tip: 1K. Amundsen collapses at warming level that is very plausible but prior ice loss from Recovery (associated with stream formation is a bit odd - similar as in 0)
- 12: H1 = 100; delta = 0.8. V at tip: 55.2 msle. f at tip: 1.3K. Similar to 6. Kind of like this one because Amundsen retreats before complete collapse of Filchner-Ronne.

The higher delta value makes quite a lot of sense: there is no way the full 16x16km cell is saturated with water. It actually represents a finer hydrological feature that represents at most 25% of the cell surface. Thus, delta = 0.75 would be a round value that does not look weird.
